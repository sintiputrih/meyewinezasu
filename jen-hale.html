Health Issues

At the age of 38, Jen Hale was diagnosed with dilated cardiomyopathy despite being a health freak, a cross-fit enthusiast, a cyclist, and a non-smoker. Cardiomyopathy is a disease that makes it harder for our hearts to pump blood to the rest of our bodies. Jen started showing the symptoms first in the summer of 2016, she complained of being exhausted all of the time and being short of breath.

Jen Hale tried to brush off the symptoms thinking it was due to her hectic schedule. Her weariness and shortness of breath started becoming more severe in September. She went to see a doctor after her body started swelling badly. Initially, she thought it was just some kind of allergy but after taking a medical checkup, she found out it was cardiomyopathy. The doctors suggested she take a break from work for 6 months to which she did not agree. After all the efforts to reach where she is today, Jen decided she would keep working.

Jen Hale wore a LifeVest to work every day for six months in addition to her medicine. The vest would track her heart’s activity and alert her if it slowed down, it would also shock her heart into action if necessary. Jen kept on working wearing LifeVest for six months. The medicine worked like a charm. Her heart was pumping at 50% capacity, she was also removed from the heart transplant list after two years. 

Jen Hale is in the best shape now, she learned to look after herself and take frequent breaks. She is actively involved with the American Heart Association after recovering from cardiomyopathy, raising awareness about heart disease. She urges people not to ignore the early symptoms of the disease.
