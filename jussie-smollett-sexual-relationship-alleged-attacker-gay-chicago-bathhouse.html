Jussie Smollett had sexual relationship with alleged attacker and met him at gay Chicago ba

DISGRACED Empire star Jussie Smollett and one of his alleged "attackers" Abimbola "Abel" Osundairo may have visited a gay Chicago bathhouse together, according to reports.

Smollett allegedly paid Osundairo, and his brother Olabinjo “Ola” Osundairo, to stage an attack on him in 2019.

The brothers both worked on the set of Empire, and Smollett is accused of paying them $3,500 to aid him, a charge he denies.

The actor faces six criminal charges, including that he planned and participated in the staging of a hate crime and made four separate false reports to the Chicago Police Department, according to Vulture.

Smollet has pleaded not guilty to all of these charges.

Now it appears Smollett, who is openly gay, may have had a "sexual relationship" with Abel, Page Six reports.

"They used to party together and he had a sexual relationship with [Abel]," a source told the outlet.

"They went to this affluent Chicago bathhouse multiple times and they had to show ID. It’s known as a bathhouse where a lot of affluent black gay men hang out."

Abel and his brother Ola have denied they are gay, and sued Smollett's lawyers last year for insinuating that there was a sexual relationship between the actor and one of the brothers.

"We have sat back and watched lie after lie being fabricated about us in the media only so one big lie can continue to have life.

"These lies are destroying our character and our reputation in our personal and professional lives," the brothers' attorney, read in a statement on their behalf at the time.

The defamation suit against Smollett's lawyers has been dismissed, and the actor continues to fight the accusation that the brothers helped him stage the hoax attack.

He says the $3,500 he gave to the Osundairos was for performance-enhancing drugs.

The brothers have told police the money was for the fake attack, which included the pair "shouting racist and homophobic slurs, dousing him with a chemical and draping a noose around his neck," prosecutors say.

Abel's friendship with Smollett dates back to 2017, according to prosecutors.

Abel had served as a stand-in for a character named “Kai”, who is Smollett’s love interest on the Empire show.

Osundairo’s brother also appeared as an extra in the show, according to the court document.

In a four-page court document describing the allegations against Smollett, prosecutors allege Smollett hired the brothers to buy masks and a rope, saying the transactions recorded were on surveillance video.

As for the alleged attack, the actor instructed Abel to “not hurt him too badly and give him a chance to appear to fight back,” according to the filing.

The Osundairos' lawyer said  the two men wanted to come clean and weren’t motivated by any promises from prosecutors.
