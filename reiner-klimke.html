Career and Professional Life

He was a Professional Equestrian from Germany. Klimke was able to appear in six Olympics from the year 1960 to the year 1988, excluding the year 1980 Games that were boycotted by West Germany. Likewise, he was able to win team gold in the years 1964, 1968 (both on Dux), 1976 (riding Mehmed), 1984, and 1988 (both with Ahlerich), and the individual gold in 1984 on Ahlerich. Moreover, his two bronze medals came in the individual event in the years 1968 and 1976.

He even had a fine record by winning six gold medals of which two were individual and four were a team at the World Championships. He was the individual champion in the years 1967, 1973, and 1985, at the European Championships. He even was able to ride on seven winning West German teams in the years 1965, 1973, 1983, 1985, and others as well.

Furthermore, He was able to compete in eventing early in his career. He also was a member of the winning West German three-day event team at the 1959 European Championships and was able to finish 18th in individual eventing at the 1960 Summer Olympics. This event made him the best German combination and he even won a Grand Prix show jumping competition in Berlin. Moreover, Reiner Klimke not only rode and trained but also ran a law firm as well as served on several boards, including the FEI Dressage Committee.
