Jimmy Butler career

Butler averaged 12.0 points per game while playing 106 games for Marquette. He was available for the 2011 NBA draft following his college graduation, and the Chicago Bulls selected him with the 30th overall choice. On December 9, 2011, he agreed to a rookie deal with the Bulls.

It started on July 1, 2011, lasted 161 days, and moved the 2011–12 season back from November 1 to December 25, 2011. Butler scored 2.6 points per game while playing 42 games in his first season with the Bulls.

Butler signed a five-year, $95 million contract with the Bulls in 2015 after they made the offer. He was given a player option for the fifth year as well. On February 5, 2016, he strained his left knee playing against the Denver Nuggets.

The next season, on October 27, 2016, he contributed significantly to the Bulls’ victory over the Boston Celtics in their season opening by scoring a team-high 24 points.

On June 22, 2017, the Bulls traded Butler to the Minnesota Timberwolves to start the 2017–18 campaign. Following that, Butler participated in 59 regular-season games for the Timberwolves.
