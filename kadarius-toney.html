Kadarius Toney is an American professional football player who has represented the New York Giants (2021-2022) and the Kansas City Chiefs (2022-Present) in the NFL (National Football League – an American football league regarded as the highest professional level of the sport in the world), as a wide receiver. He had been picked 20th overall in the 1st round of the 2021 NFL Draft by the New York Giants. With the Kansas City Chiefs, Kadarius won Super Bowl LVII (the championship game of the 2022 NFL season). In that game (played on February 12, 2023), he set a new Super Bowl record with a 65-yard punt return. The Kansas City Chiefs went on to defeat the Philadelphia Eagles by a 38-35 margin to win the Super Bowl for the 3rd time. This was the team’s 5th Super Bowl appearance overall and their 3rd in the last 4 seasons.

  • In his senior year (2020) with the University of Florida, Kadarius led the Florida Gators’ offense with 70 receptions for 984 yards – scoring 10 touchdowns. In 11 starts for the team, he also contributed as a runner (8.5 yards per carry and 1 touchdown) and returner (7 kick returns, 11 punt returns, and 1 touchdown). For his performances, he was included in the ‘First-Team All-SEC‘.
  • The SEC (Southeastern Conference) is a college athletic conference whose member institutions are located primarily in the South Central and Southeastern United States.   • In the New York Giants‘ ‘Week 5’ game of the 2021 NFL season, against the Dallas Cowboys, Kadarius recorded 10 catches for 189 yards. This broke Odell Beckham Jr.‘s franchise receiving record for a rookie.
