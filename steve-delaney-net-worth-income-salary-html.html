In addition to his work as a comedian and character actor, Steve Delaney (born 1955) is most known for creating the fictitious comic character of Count Arthur Strong. In Leeds, Delaney was born to a foundryman’s family as the son of a foundryman. He dropped out of school to work on a market booth while also participating in amateur dramatics. After taking theatrical courses and spending time at art school, he worked as an assistant stage manager at the Grand Theatre and the Leeds Playhouse in his hometown of Leeds, and then as a theatre carpenter at the Northcott Theatre in the city of Exeter. In the 1980s, he went to the Central School of Speech and Drama in London to further his education.
