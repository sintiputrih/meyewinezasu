How did Cory Spinks start his Professional Career?

Cory Spinks’ professional boxing career began when he was 19 years old. He had a successful amateur career, winning regional, national, and international tournaments, including the National Golden Gloves titles in 1995 and 1997.

Before his first loss in 2001, he had won his previous 26 professional fights since going pro in 1997. Spinks won the IBF welterweight title in 2003, his first world title, by defeating Michele Piccirillo. He then defeated Ricardo Mayorga in 2004 to win the undisputed welterweight title.

After that, he moved up to junior middleweight, where he won the IBF championship in 2006. He successfully defended the championship three times before losing it to Verno Phillips in 2008. Furthermore, he retired from professional boxing in 2013 with a record of 39 wins, 8 losses, and 0 draws. He was one of only a few fighters to hold uncontested titles in two distinct weight classes, and he was well known for his defensive prowess and boxing IQ.
