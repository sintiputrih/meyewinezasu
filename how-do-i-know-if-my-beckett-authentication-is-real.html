How do I know if my Beckett Authentication is real?

Beckett Authentication Services (BAS) is a leading sports authentication company that provides authentication services for sports memorabilia and collectibles. They use state-of-the-art technology and a team of expert authenticators to verify the authenticity of sports-related items.

However, with the rise of counterfeit products in the market, it is important to know how to verify the authenticity of your Beckett Authentication. Here are some tips to help you know if your Beckett Authentication is real:

  • Check the hologram: Every Beckett-certified item comes with a unique hologram that contains a serial number. This hologram is placed on the item by the authenticator and is a clear sign of authenticity. You can verify the authenticity of the hologram by checking the serial number on the Beckett website or by using their mobile app.

  • Look for the Certificate of Authenticity (COA): Every Beckett-certified item comes with a COA, which is a document that contains information about the item and the authentication process. The COA is signed by the authenticator and is another clear sign of authenticity. You should always check the COA to verify the authenticity of the item.

  • Check the item against known examples: If you are unsure about the authenticity of your item, you can compare it to known examples of similar items. You can do this by looking at online auction sites or by consulting with experts in the field. By comparing your item to known examples, you can identify any discrepancies that may indicate that your item is not authentic.

  • Get a second opinion: If you are still unsure about the authenticity of your item, you can get a second opinion from another authenticator. There are many authenticators who specialize in different areas of sports memorabilia, and they can provide you with an unbiased opinion about the authenticity of your item.

  • Beware of counterfeit items: Be aware that there are many counterfeit items in the market that are made to look like Beckett-certified items. These items may have fake holograms and COAs, and can be difficult to identify. To avoid purchasing counterfeit items, only buy from reputable dealers who have a proven track record of selling authentic items.

    • In conclusion, knowing how to verify the authenticity of your Beckett Authentication is important in protecting your investment in sports memorabilia and collectibles. By following these tips, you can ensure that your item is authentic and avoid the disappointment of purchasing a counterfeit item.
