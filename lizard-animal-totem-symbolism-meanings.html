Symbolic Meaning Of Lizard

Lizards possess one of the most remarkable and unique sets of defense mechanisms in the animal kingdom. Not only can they move in a way that makes it difficult to be caught: but they are also experts in camouflage, utilizing trickery to confuse their predators and remain safe. Even this is not the highlight of their defense system, though.

When a predator catches the lizards, they can shed their tail to escape, leaving their captor completely boggled long enough to scurry away under rocks or into the jungle. Of course, they can grow it back with time, but the act is still symbolic of quite a sacrifice in the name of survival. This act is a literal symbol of regeneration and shows us that we can symbolically do this ourselves. The new tail is also a symbol of growth and rebirth.
