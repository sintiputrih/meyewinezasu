Career

Earlier Work & Television

When she was 8 or 9 months old, Rivera was represented by the same talent agent as her mother, who had moved to Los Angeles to pursue modeling. She started her career as a child model for a commercial of KMart. As a child, she was seen in numerous advertisements.

Naya Rivera in an Advertisement

In 1991, she made her television debut in the role of ‘Hillary Winston’ in the series ‘The Royal Family.’

Naya Rivera in The Royal Family

She then appeared in small roles in the TV series: The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air (1993), Family Matters (1992-93), Live Shot (1995), Baywatch (1996), Smart Guy (1997-99), House Blend (2002), and Even Stevens(2002). In 2006 and 2007, she acted in the musical ‘U Don’t Know Me’ in Los Angeles and National Tours. In 2009, she auditioned for the American series ‘Glee’ and was cast as the cheerleader ‘Santana Lopez.’ The role became a breakthrough in her career. With the show, she gained immense popularity. In the first season of the show, she was a recurring character and from the second season, her character became the main one.

Naya Rivera in Glee (2009)

She has acted in the TV shows: Devious Maids (2015), American Dad! (2016), and Step-Up: High Water (2018-19).

She worked in the films: Frankenhood (2009), Glee: The 3D Concert Movie (2011), The Naughty List (2011), and Mad Families (2017).

As a Singer & Other Works

She sang many tracks for the Glee Series. In 2012, she was featured as the vocalist in the song ‘Supermassive Black Hole’ by 2Cellos.

In 2011, she signed a record deal with Columbia Records. She released her first single ‘Sorry’ with Big Sean in 2013.

Rivera released a six-track EP titled ‘My Heart’ in April 2019 and contributed to the original soundtrack for Step Up: High Water.

She published a memoir ‘Sorry Not Sorry: Dreams, Mistakes, and Growing Up’ in 2016.

Disappearance and Death

On July 8, 2020, she was declared ‘missing’ after her four-year-old son, Josey, was found alone in the boat rented by Naya at Lake Piru. Three hours after they left the dock, the search for then began as their rental was over and they had not returned. A boater discovered Josey sleeping alone on the boat with a life jacket on. Josey told investigators that he and his mother were swimming and she helped him climb back into the boat but she was unable to climb on the boat and then disappeared underwater. On July 9, the Ventura County Sheriff’s Office confirmed to NBC that Rivera was presumed dead and that rescue efforts would now shift to recovery efforts. On July 13, a body was found floating at Lake Piru, which was confirmed to be Naya, and she was officially declared dead. On July 14, the Ventura County medical examiner released an autopsy report, which stated that the cause of death was an accidental drowning and that there was no evidence of injury or intoxication.
